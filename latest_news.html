SNU Data Science Seminar “AI and Control Theory for Safety in Aviation Autonomy” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, Apr 23, 2025 Zoom: https://snu-ac-kr.zoom.us/j/2507466555 Abstract Decision-making in aviation involves many complex layers—from operational-level decisions like route scheduling, to trajectory-level decisions such as air traffic control, to vehicle-level decisions like flight stabilization. In this talk, I will focus on emerging safety challenges across these layers and explore how tools from machine leaming and control theory can help address them. Specifically, I will introduce two projects that combine control-theoretic tools for safety guarantees with data-driven approaches for ensuring safety at both the vehicle dynamics level and the air traffic control level. First, I will introduce a safety challenge in multi-agent scenarios—known as the “leaky comer” issue—and demonstrate how we can resolve it to achieve Safe MARL for automated ATM by integrating MARL with safety certificate functions from control theory. Next, I will present the concept ofa Data-Driven Hamiltonian, which integrates data with control objectives to provide safety guarantees. Jason Jangho Choi Jason Jangho Choi is a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the University of California, Berkeley. He received his B.Sc in Mechanical Engineering from Seoul National University, Korea, in 2019. His research focuses on safety assurance for leaming-enabled decision-making in dynamical systems, with applications in robotics and aviation autonomy. He has collaborated with several leading institutions in the aviation industry, including NASA and Joby Aviation.
