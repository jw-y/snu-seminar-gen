SNU Data Science Seminar “Accelerating Semiconductor Innovation: The Integration of Generative Models and AI ‘ 4 98 in Chip Design Time : 09:00 - 10:30, Mar 18, 2026 Zoom: https://snu-ac-kr.zoom.us/j/2507466555 Abstract Modem semiconductor design is facing increasing complexity that challenges the scaling limits of traditional electronic design automation (EDA) heuristics. Over the past decade, predictive machine learning (ML) has been progressively adopted in the industry to improve design Quality of Results (QoR) and reduce tumaround times. Building on this foundation, the field is now exploring the direct integration of Generative Al, Large Language Models (LLMs), and Reinforcement Learning into the hardware design workflow. This seminar will examine how these recent AI methodologies are applied to core chip design problems. The presentation will cover topics ranging from automated RTL (Register-Transfer Level) generation using domain-adapted LLMs to standard cell layout optimization driven by reinforcement learning and reasoning agents. Furthermore, the talk will address the underlying data science challenges specific to EDA, including the scarcity of open-source hardware training data, tokenization of hardware description languages, and the complexities of representing physical circuit netlists. Through a review of recent literature and industrial frameworks, this presentation will outline how data-driven approaches are moving beyond conventional algorithms to advance semiconductor development.
