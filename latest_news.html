SNU Data Science Seminar “Vector-Centric Machine Learning Systems: A Cross-Stack Approach” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, Nov 5, 2025 Zoom: https://snu-ac-kr.zoom.us/j Abstract Despite the recent popularity of large language models (LLMs), the transformer neural network invented eight years ago has remained largely unchanged. It prompts the question of whether machine learning (ML) systems research is solely about improving hardware and software for tensor operations. In this talk, I will argue that the future of machine leaning systems extends far beyond model acceleration. Using the increasingly popular retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) paradigm as an example, I will show that the growing complexity of ML systems demands a deeply collaborative effort spanning data management. systems, computer architecture, and ML. To illustrate this vision, I will discuss RAGO and Chameleon, two recent systems that explore performance optimization and heterogeneous acceleration for RAG workloads. 2507466555 Wendi Jiang Wengi Jiang is an incoming Assistant Professor at the National University of Singapore. He recently received his PhD at ETH Zurich, advised by Gustavo Alonso and Torsten Hoefler. He aims to advance next-generation machine learning systems through research at the intersection of data management, computer systems, and computer architecture. His work has driven advancements in retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), vector search, and recommender systems.
