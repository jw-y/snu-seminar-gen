SNU Data Science Seminar “Why Humanoids Now?” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, Jun 4, 2025 Zoom: https://snu- Abstract As automation accelerates in manufacturing and logistics, the demand for robots that can collaborate with humans and handle tasks involving interaction and cooperation is rapidly growing. In response, companies such as Boston Dynamics, Tesla, and Figure are intensifying their efforts in humanoid robot development, drawing renewed attention to the field. This talk explores the factors driving this resurgence by looking at key technological advances, historical developments, and real-world applications. It will share insights on designing human-friendly robots, with a focus on motion control and human-robot interaction that enable safe and seamless integration into human environments. The talk also highlights the retum of teleoperation and its significance, illustrated through recent research and KIMLAB’s approaches. In the era of Embodied and Physical Al, this talk aims to offer a perspective on how humanoid robots can more naturally blend into our daily lives—and why now is the time to focus on their development and deployment. Joohyung Kim Joohyung Kim is an Associate Professor of ECE (Electrical and Computer Engineering) and MechSE (Mechanical Science & Engineering), and the director of KIMLAB (Kinetic Intelligent Machine LAB) at University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign. His research focuses on design and control for humanoid robots, system for motion learning in robot hardware. and safe human- robot interaction. He received BSE and Ph.D. degrees in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS) from Seoul National University, Korea, in 2001 and 2012.
