SNU Data Science Seminar “Towards Scalable and Open Multimodal AI for Biomedical Understanding” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, Apr 1, 2026 Zoom: https://snu-ac-kr.zoom.us/j/2507466555 Abstract The development of foundation models is driven by large-scale datasets. However, progress toward generalist biomedical vision-language models (VLMs) is limited by the lack of publicly accessible datasets across biology and medicine. To address this gap, we introduce BIOMEDICA (the open Biomedical Image-Caption Archive): a scalable, open-source framework to extract, annotate, and serialize the entirety of the PubMed Central Open Access subset into an easy-to-use and accessible dataset. Our framework produces a comprehensive archive with over 24 million unique image-text pairs from over 6 million articles, supplemented by rich metadata and expert-guided annotations. I will share the technical and engineering insights gained from building this archive, the performance of our state-of-the-art BMCA-CLIP models across 40 diverse biomedical tasks, and our recent work extending to long-context modeling. Min Woo Sun Min Woo Sun is a PhD candidate at the Stanford Attificial Intelligence Laboratory (SAIL), advised by Serena Yeung-Levy and Robert Tibshirani. A Stanford Data Science Scholar and MAC3 Fellow, his research focuses on building large-scale open-source datasets and vision-language models to create reproducible, clinically meaningful AI.
