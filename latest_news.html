SNU Data Science Seminar “Toward the Integration of Machine Learning and Adaptive Optical Microscopy” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, May 21, 2025 -kr.zoom.us/j/2507466 Zoom: https://snu-ac 5 Abstract Optical microscopy provides high-resolution images of subcellular structures in vivo, yet imaging deep brain regions (which is essential for studying neuronal interactions via dendrites and axons) is hampered by tissue heterogeneity and absorption. As light passes through inhomogeneous tissue, optical aberrations degrade resolution and contrast, while absorption lowers signal-to-noise ratio (SNR). Although adaptive optics (AO) can correct these nonidealities, it increases hardware complexity and cost, hindering its systematic integration. In this talk, I will introduce physics-informed machine-learning-based AO frameworks for widefield and two-photon fluorescence microscopy that achieve high-resolution, high-contrast imaging under deep-brain aberrations and low SNRs with minimal additional hardware. Iksung Kang Iksung Kang, Ph.D., is a postdoctoral fellow in the Departments of Physics and Neuroscience at the University of California, Berkeley. He earned his BS. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Seoul National University in 2017 and his M.S. and Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 2020 and 2022, respectively, under the supervision of Professor George Barbastathis.
