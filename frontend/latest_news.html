SNU Data Science Seminar “User Simulation via Language Models” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, Jun 10, 2026 Zoom: https://snu-ac-kr.zoom.us/j/2507466555 Abstract Large language models (LLMs) offer compelling means to simulate diverse human users, capturing how they engage in conversations, express opinions, and make decisions in context. Yet achieving coherent, realistic binding between an individual human and their counterpart LLM virtual persona remains poorly understood. In this talk, I present research advancing methodology and evaluation for LLMs as high-fidelity virtual personas. First, I present empirical results supporting the argument that user simulation is distinct from assistant alignment: pretrained base LLMs are naturally human-language mixture models, while instruction-tuned models systematically fail as simulated users. In response, I propose a methodology of pairing a base LLM generator with an instruction-tuned selector that retains human-likeness in produced utterances without sacrificing distributional realism. I then discuss how naturalistic life narratives (“backstories”) condition LLMs as virtual personas far more effectively than coarse demographic profiles, improving representativeness, consistency, and behavioral realism. Next, 1 demonstrate that this matters for deep, rather than merely shallow, user binding: backstory-conditioned personas reproduce known in-group/out-group effects from human studies, which previous methods of instructing or steering models toward target populations fail to capture. Together, these works lay principled foundations for building and evaluating LLMs as synthetic users, with direct applications to conversational AI training and evaluation. His research focuses on Natural Language Processing and large language models. with particular emphasis on user simulation, virtual personas, and building collaborative AI agents.
