SNU Data Science Seminar “Scaling Multimodal Intelligence in Precision Medicine” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, May 13, 2026 Zoom: b Abstract AI systems that lean from multimodal signals across genome, exposome, and screenome are increasingly central to precision medicine, offering the possibility of delivering deeply personalized, timely care. This vision hinges on two unresolved challenges: the reliable translation of intensive heterogeneous data into actionable insights and the efficient deployment of complex models within resource-constrained mobile/wearable devices. In this talk, I will introduce a novel framework that leverages adaptive fusion of multimodal streaming data and advances in edge parallel computing to address these challenges. I present several recent projects that apply this framework to real-world healthcare settings, highlighting both its promise and its current limitations. Finally, 1 will discuss ongoing collaborative efforts to scale precision medicine through open-source initiatives. He joined Stanford University in 2023 as a Senior AI & Data Science Lead of the Human Screenome Project and is currently a Postdoctoral Scholar in Psychology /Pediatrics.
