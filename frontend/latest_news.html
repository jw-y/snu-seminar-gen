SNU Data Science Seminar “Toward Adaptive AI Models Continual Learning in Practice” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, May 20, 2026 Zoom: https://snu-ac-kr.zoom.us/j/2507466555 Abstract Continual Learning (CL) enables models to learn continuously without forgetting, but a significant gap exists between conventional academic settings and real-world deployment. In this talk, I will bridge this gap by introducing practical perspectives on CL. First, I will highlight critical flaws in conventional CL evaluation protocols, specifically unrealistic hyperparameter tuning assumptions, and present a more generalizable evaluation protocol. Second, I will challenge the traditional exemplar- restricted CL paradigm. Assuming a realistic regime where storage is abundant but GPU compute is the primary bottleneck, I will introduce a novel approach that prioritizes cost efficiency and plasticity. Finally, 1 will briefly discuss my other research in machine unlearning, knowledge distillation, and alignment, high! their roles in building reliable and adaptable AI models.
