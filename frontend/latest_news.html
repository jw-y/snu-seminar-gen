SNU Data Science Seminar “The Data Scientist's Workflow: Experimentation, Decision-Making, and Working Alongside AI” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, May 27, 2026 Zoom: https://snu-ac-kr.zoom.us/j/2507466555 Abstract What does a data scientist actually do in industry, and how is that workflow changing as AI reshapes every step of the job? In this talk, I share a practitioner's view from nearly two years at Moloco, an AI performance advertising company. I begin with what an industry data science team's work looks like end-to-end: defining the right problem, navigating large-scale messy data, designing and validating analyses, communicating with engineering and product partners, and ultimately influencing decisions. I then focus on experimentation, where my team works on controlled experiments at scale, using A/B testing and metric design as running examples. 1 close with reflections on how the rise of capable AI assistants has changed the day-to-day practice of data science: what has become cheaper, what has become harder, and which skills have become more valuable, not less. Jayoon Jang Jayoon Jang is a Data Scientist on the Experimentation Platform team at Moloco, an AI performance advertising company. Her team builds the methodology and infrastructure that lets product and engineering teams across the company run controlled experiments at scale and support data-driven product decisions. She received her B.S. in Statistics and Mathematical Sciences from Seoul National University in 2019. and her PhD. in Statistics from Stanford University in 2024.
