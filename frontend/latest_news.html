SNU Data Science Seminar “Human-Centered Visual Analytics for Discovery, Transparency, and Storytelling in the Age of AI and Agents” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, Apr 15, 2026 Zoom: https://snu-ac-kr.zoom.us/j/2507466555 Abstract The ever-growing volume of data presents both remarkable opportunities and significant challenges. While AI models excel at identifying pattems, their decision-making processes often remain opaque and difficult to interpret. My research focuses on designing human- centered visual analytics systems that bridge this gap, enabling users to navigate complex data landscapes while effectively leveraging Al's capabilities. In this talk, I will highlight design studies that showcase how these systems facilitate knowledge discovery in biomedicine through close collaboration with domain experts. Beyond this, I will explore how visual analytics can be used to interrogate AI biases and enhance model explainability, fostering fairness and transparency. Additionally, I am passionate about unlocking the storytelling potential of data visualization by developing intuitive authoring tools and addressing barriers to visualization literacy. Ultimately, my goal is to democratize data understanding, empowering individuals across disciplines to extract meaningful insights and communicate them effectively. I will conclude by sharing my vision for future research directions in this dynamic and evolving field. Bum Chul Kwon Bum Chul Kwon is a Staff Research Scientist on IBM Research’s Al for Healthcare and Life Sciences team, and an adjunct professor teaching graduate-level data visualization courses at Columbia University. the University of Pennsylvania, and the University of Califomia, Berkeley. His research bridges human-centered design and data science, with a focus on visual analytics for Al-driven discovery, transparency, and storytelling in health and life sciences.
