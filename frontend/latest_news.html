SNU Data Science Seminar “Multimodal Data for Robot Manipulation: From Tactile Sensing to Scalable Human Demonstrations” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, Apr 8, 2026 Zoom: https://snu-ac-kr.zoom.us/j/2507466: Abstract Robots are rapidly advancing toward real-world deployment, yet they continue to struggle with everyday tasks involving contact-rich manipulation. One critical but underexplored bottleneck is multimodal robot data; not just large-scale vision, but data that captures the physics of interaction, including contact forces. In this talk, I will present my work on acquiring multimodal data for robots at scale, enabling generalization across scenes and tasks while interacting with objects both delicately and forcefully as needed. 1 will introduce CoinFT, a coin-sized, low-cost six-axis force-torque sensor for diverse robot platforms, and UMI-FT, a handheld device for scalable collection of multimodal human demonstrations. I will also discuss ongoing efforts at Sunday Robotics to bring useful robots into our homes through large-scale demonstration data collection, and promising directions for fusing multimodal data to improve robot performance. Hojung Choi Dr. Hojung Choi is a Research Engineer at Sunday Robotics, a unicom robotics startup in Silicon Valley. His research focuses on advancing robot manipulation through multimodal sensing, novel hardware. and AL. Prior to Sunday Robotics, he was a postdoctoral researcher at the Robotics and Embodied AI Lab (REAL) at Stanford University, advised by Prof. Shuran Song. He holds a PhD in Mechanical Engineering from Stanford University, advised by Prof. Mark Cutkosky in the Biomimetics and Dexterous Manipulation Lab. His work has been published in venues including Science Advances, T- RO, RA-L, ICRA, and IROS.
