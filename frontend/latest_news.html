SNU Data Science Seminar “Rethinking Quantization for LLM Efficiency: Achieving Zero Overhead at Ultra-Low Bitwidths” Time : 09:00 - 10:30, Apr 22, 2026 Zoom: https://snu-ac-kr.zoom.us/j/2507466555 Abstract As LLMs continue to scale in context length, the memory bottleneck caused by the Key- Value (KV) cache has emerged as a primary barrier to efficient inference. While quantization is a standard approach to mitigate this, conventional methods often suffer from significant precision loss at ultra-low bitwidths and hidden memory overheads due to per-block quantization constants. In this seminar, we explore a transformative approach to quantization that achieves near-lossless performance without metadata overhead. We begin by discussing the theoretical foundations of Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss (QJL) and PolarQuant, which introduced the concept of data-oblivious, unbiased estimation through random rotations. By transforming the distribution of activations into a mathematically tractable form, these techniques allow for a "zero-overhead" architecture where bit-savings translate directly into physical memory reduction. Furthermore, we will discuss how these principles were integrated into TurboQuant, a recent breakthrough with Google Research. TurboQuant demonstrates that by combining structural MSE-optimization with unbiased vector quantization, it is possible to reduce KV cache memory by a factor of 6x while maintaining downstream accuracy in long-context benchmarks. Insu Han Insu Han is an Assistant Professor in the School of Electrical Engineering at KAIST. Currently, he is also serving as a Visiting Faculty Researcher at Google Research. Prior to joining KAIST, he was an Applied Scientist at Adobe Firefly and a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Yale Institute for Foundations of Data Science (FDS). He eamed his PhD., M.S., and B.S. in Electrical Engineering from KAIST. His research focuses on developing efficient and scalable algorithms for AI foundation models by bridging rigorous theory and practical optimization.
